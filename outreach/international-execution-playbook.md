# PixelorCode — International Client KILL CHAIN (Execution Playbook)
_v1 · 2026-06-12 · Pairs with international-client-strategy.md (macro) + international-email-sequences.md (copy) + CRM list "US-AU Health — Jun 2026" (746 leads, 205 with emails)._

## The math we're engineering around
205 emailable × 3% reply = ~6 replies → ~2 calls → 0-1 close. Too thin alone. Multipliers:
1. CHANNELS: contact forms (541 no-email leads; zero spam risk) + FB/IG DMs (152 social-as-website leads live there) + LinkedIn
2. RESPONSE: Loom + named mockup = 3-5x text; evidence-led opener = 2-3x generic
3. TRUST: kill the "random offshore agency" objection before it forms (Stage 1)

## STAGE 1 — TRUST FLOOR (week 1, ₹0, Vansh + Rishav)
A US dentist who Googles "PixelorCode" must find, before any email lands:
- [ ] Ryan's Tuition Centre case study page on pixelorcode.com (English, screens, outcomes)
- [ ] "Websites for Dental & Health Practices" page with PUBLIC USD pricing:
      Practice Site $2,500 · Site + Online Booking $3,500 · Care Plan $250/mo
- [ ] 2 spec dental concepts, honestly labeled "Concept — Austin TX practice"
- [ ] Founders' LinkedIn: faces, niche headline ("Websites & booking systems for health practices")
- [ ] FOUNDING CLIENT OFFER: "First 3 US practices: the $3,500 build for $1,900 + your honest review."
      Not cheap — early. Retires publicly after client #3. This manufactures US case studies.

## STAGE 2 — SNIPER 50 (agent deep-verify; Gorakhpur standard)
Per lead: still operating? · truly no website anywhere (search, not just data)? · owner name from THEIR pages only · Google rating/review count · best channel (email/form/DM). Output: 50 bulletproof dossiers. No send until its dossier exists.

## STAGE 3 — TRIPLE-TOUCH ASSAULT (daily ritual ~90 min once inbox warm)
5 leads/day deep, not 50 shallow:
- Vansh (IST morning): 5 named mockups from master template (~10 min each after first)
- Rishav (IST evening = US morning): 5 verdict-matched emails + 5 contact-form submissions + 5 LinkedIn connects; Loom for every 4.8★+ lead
- Follow-ups D3/D7/D14 BEFORE new sends (follow-ups produce most replies)
- AU leads: same motion, IST morning
- Log every touch in CRM same day (status, next_follow_up +3d)

## STAGE 4 — THE CALL (15 min, their morning = your evening)
Goal: SHOW, don't sell. Script:
1. (2 min) "Before this call I looked at how patients find you — can I share my screen?"
2. (3 min) Their broken thing, live. Calm, factual, no gloating.
3. (5 min) The mockup with THEIR name. "This is yours either way."
4. (3 min) "Live in 3 weeks. Founding price $1,900 — half to start, half at launch."
5. (2 min) "Want me to send the agreement today?"

Objections:
| They say | You say |
|---|---|
| "Where are you based?" | "We're a design + engineering studio working globally — here's our work. Our timezone is your superpower: changes you ask for at 5pm are done before your 9am." (NEVER fake a US address) |
| "How do I know you're legit?" | "Fair. Don't pay anything today — we start on a signed scope and a deposit through PayPal, which protects you. And here's our founding-client deal: your review is half the payment." |
| "Too cheap — what's the catch?" | "It's a founding price for our first three US practices, in exchange for a review. Client #4 pays $3,500." |
| "I need to think" | "Totally fine. The mockup's yours. One question — is it the price, the timing, or trusting us? I'd rather fix the real thing." |
| "Can you also do SEO/ads?" | "We build the foundation first. Care plan covers updates + local SEO basics; ads partners come later." |

## STAGE 5 — CLOSE & COLLECT
- PayPal Business invoice for the deposit (trust > the 4.4% fee). Wise Business later for better rates.
- 1-page SOW: scope, 3-week timeline, 2 revision rounds, 50/50 payment. DEPOSIT = CLOSED.
- Deliver in 3 weeks → launch email → ask Google review + case-study permission within 7 days of launch.
- Every client → $250/mo care plan pitch at launch ("we keep it fast, secure, updated, and tweak monthly").

## 30-day calendar

- Miss = diagnose the leaking stage only: no replies → message/deliverability · replies-no-calls → trust floor · calls-no-close → offer.

## Operating principle
We do not sell websites. We deliver a VERDICT: "Here is the specific broken thing costing you patients; here is your fixed version with your name on it; here's the price; we start this week." Verdict sellers close SMBs. Explorers don't.
